The Crisis of Algorithm Fatigue: Why Social Media is Broken in India

For creators in India, the promise of social media has yielded a relentless cycle of algorithmic burnout. Mainstream advertising feeds dictate engagement, reducing genuine expression to a metric and fostering a pervasive sense of isolation amidst constant digital noise. This ceaseless demand to perform for an algorithm, rather than connect with humans, paradoxically deepens loneliness. The platforms designed to bridge distances now often widen the chasm between individuals, creating a superficial digital landscape where authentic interaction is increasingly scarce.

This digital fatigue fuels a palpable yearning for authenticity: a desire to discover genuine neighborhood places, to cultivate physical third places for real-world connection, and to share passions without the burden of public validation. Consider the quiet satisfaction of sharing a high-fidelity Apple Music playlist with a select few, where the value lies in the curation itself, not in public like counts. This deliberate act of sharing, unburdened by performance pressure, illustrates a core human need for meaningful exchange over performative display. Traditional social media, with its endless scrolls and commercial imperatives, fails to deliver this nuanced, high-fidelity human experience.
