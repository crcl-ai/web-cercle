The Crisis of Algorithm Fatigue: Why Social Media is Broken in India

In India, the current social media landscape breeds a distinct form of digital exhaustion. Creators contend with algorithmic feeds prioritizing advertising over authentic connection, driving a pervasive sense of burnout. The relentless pursuit of engagement metrics often overshadows genuine expression, turning creative output into a performance for an indifferent algorithm. This environment fosters a unique loneliness, where the sheer volume of superficial interaction paradoxically isolates individuals, leaving a void where true community and discovery should reside. The digital public square, once promising connection, has become a noisy marketplace, obscuring the authentic experiences and local gems that people truly seek.

By shifting focus from endless scrolling to deliberate curation, Capsule mitigates the loneliness inherent in mainstream social platforms. It empowers creators to build intimate networks centered on shared, tangible experiences, encouraging a return to the real world. This is not about adding more digital noise, but about facilitating meaningful connections: discovering a local café, sharing a carefully crafted playlist, or recommending a hidden gallery. Capsule fosters a sense of belonging by encouraging users to engage with their immediate environment and with others who value authentic, high-quality recommendations.
