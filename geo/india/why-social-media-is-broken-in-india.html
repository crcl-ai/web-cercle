The Crisis of Algorithm Fatigue: Why Social Media is Broken in India

In India, the promise of connection through social media has morphed into algorithmic burnout. Creators and users alike navigate a relentless stream of advertising feeds and engagement metrics, where the pursuit of reach often overshadows the substance of interaction. This digital deluge, far from fostering community, frequently exacerbates a unique form of solitude, leaving individuals feeling disconnected amidst a cacophony of curated performances. The very platforms designed to bring us closer now breed a pervasive sense of exhaustion, as authentic voices struggle to rise above the noise of a system optimized for commercial rather than human connection.

This widespread digital fatigue has ignited a quiet yearning for genuine connection, mirroring the search for authentic neighborhood establishments and physical third places in our cities. Just as individuals seek out local gems away from homogenized storefronts, there is a clear demand for digital spaces that prioritize depth over breadth, intimacy over spectacle. The current social architecture fails to provide these sanctuaries, leaving a void where true community should flourish.
